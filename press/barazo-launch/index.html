Barazo: Federated forums on the AT Protocol

A forum platform where your identity and data actually belong to you.

Barazo is a federated forum built on the AT Protocol. Your account and posts live on your PDS, not locked inside each community. One identity across every forum, and your contributions follow you.

For community managers

Use our managed hosting or self-host for free. The entire stack is open source: AGPL for the backend, MIT for the frontend, lexicons, and deployment templates. Host it wherever you want.

Your members already have accounts. Anyone with an AT Protocol identity (Bluesky, or any other app on the protocol) can participate immediately, no "create yet another account" step. You bring one community online, and everyone who's already on the protocol walks right in.

For members

Portable identity across every Barazo community, cross-community reputation that builds over time as you contribute, and real data ownership: your posts live in your PDS, so you can take them with you if you leave.

No more creating separate accounts for every forum. No more losing your post history when one shuts down.

Why now

Social networks are filling up with AI-generated content, and the signal-to-noise ratio keeps getting worse. Communities need places where real human contributions are tied to real, verifiable identities, and the AT Protocol makes that possible at the protocol level.

Governments and organizations across Europe, Canada, and beyond are also moving away from platform dependency. Barazo is built for that world: open source, self-hostable, no lock-in.

Part of Singi Labs

Barazo is one of two products from Singi Labs. Its sibling Sifa is a professional identity layer for the AT Protocol that takes real activity from across the ecosystem and shows it as a verifiable professional profile. Activity in Barazo communities builds your Sifa profile, and Sifa's trust infrastructure (sybil detection, anti-abuse labels) protects Barazo communities in return.
