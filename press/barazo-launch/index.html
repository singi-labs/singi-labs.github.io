Barazo: Federated forums on the AT Protocol

A forum platform where your identity and data actually belong to you.

Barazo is a federated forum built on the AT Protocol. Your account and posts live on your PDS (personal data server), not locked inside each community. One identity across every forum, and your contributions follow you.

Barazo is in open alpha. It works, it has rough edges, and we're building in public. This is the right moment to get in if you want to help shape it.

Barazo staging instance: discussions with AT Protocol identities

Self-host it today

Everything is open source: AGPL for the backend, MIT for the frontend, lexicons, and deployment templates. Grab the Docker Compose template and host it wherever you want.

Your members already have accounts. Anyone with a Bluesky or AT Protocol identity can join without creating a new profile. You bring one community online, and everyone who's already on the protocol walks right in.

Managed hosting is in development. Join the waitlist if you want us to run it for you.

For members

Portable identity across every Barazo community and real data ownership: your posts live in your PDS, so you can take them with you if you leave. If Barazo ever shuts down, your data doesn't disappear with it.

No more creating separate accounts for every forum. No more losing your post history when a community closes.

Why now

Social networks are filling up with AI-generated content, and the signal-to-noise ratio keeps getting worse. Communities need places where real human contributions are tied to real, verifiable identities. The AT Protocol makes that possible.

Governments and organizations across Europe, Canada, and beyond are moving away from platform dependency. Barazo is built for that world: open source, self-hostable, no lock-in.

"I built Barazo because I wanted a forum where my community's data wasn't someone else's business model. The AT Protocol makes that technically possible now. The rest is execution."
— Guido Jansen, founder

Where this goes

Managed hosting is coming in Q3 2026 for community managers who don't want to run their own infrastructure. Same open source stack, we just handle the ops. Join the waitlist if that's you.

We're also working on a plugin system, an explore directory to discover communities across the network, and deeper integration with Sifa's trust infrastructure as it matures.

Part of Singi Labs

Barazo is one of two products from Singi Labs, built by Guido Jansen in the Netherlands. Its sibling Sifa is a professional identity layer for the AT Protocol. As Barazo communities grow, your forum contributions will appear as verified activity on your Sifa professional profile. And Sifa's trust infrastructure (sybil detection, anti-abuse labels) feeds back into Barazo to help keep communities safe.

Open source (AGPL + MIT). Based in the EU, built to be self-hosted anywhere.
