Sifa ID: Professional identity on the AT Protocol

Your profile, powered by what you actually do in your communities.

The problem we could not ignore

Professional profiles are broken. They are flat, static, and entirely self-reported. You list your job titles, you write your own summary, you collect endorsements from people who may have never seen your work. And then, because none of that is connected to anything real, the only way to stay visible is to post performative content on the platform itself. Thought leadership that nobody asked for. AI-generated thinkpieces spewed into the void.

Meanwhile, the real conversations are happening somewhere else. In community forums, open source projects, working groups, Slack channels. The places where you actually help people, share what you know, and build things. None of that shows up on your professional profile. It just disappears.

And then the platform asks you to pay to reach your own followers. You built those connections. Your colleagues, your community, your network. And now you need to boost your post to show up in their feed. That feels like betrayal.

What Sifa does differently

Sifa ID is a professional identity layer built on the AT Protocol. Sign in with your Bluesky account and claim your profile at sifa.id.

Your profile is not a form you fill out once and forget. It is alive. Your Bluesky posts already appear as your activity feed. As AT Protocol apps grow and Barazo communities come online, your real contributions in those communities flow into your profile automatically. Verified against your cryptographic identity. Not self-reported. Not curated. Just: here is what this person actually did.

Want to bring your existing career history? Import your LinkedIn profile in your browser. The data never leaves your device -- it goes straight to your PDS, your personal data server on the AT Protocol.

Sifa ID profile with career history, community activity feed, and track record

Community-powered, not individual

Traditional professional networks are built around the individual. You, alone, polishing your profile. Sifa flips that. Your profile gets stronger because of your communities and their interactions with your contributions. You help someone in a forum. You write a guide that people actually use. You answer questions, start discussions, build things. That activity is your professional reputation, and it speaks louder than any self-written summary.

Post where it matters -- in the communities where the work happens -- and let your profile reflect that. Instead of performing for an algorithm, contribute to your community. Sifa makes sure it counts.

"I got tired of a professional network that rewards you for posting into the void instead of contributing to your community. The people doing real work in real communities deserve a profile that reflects that."
-- Guido Jansen, founder

Why now

The AT Protocol ecosystem is now large enough to build on. 25 million people already have an identity on the protocol. The developer tooling matured in early 2026. And nobody has published professional networking schemas for it yet. Sifa is first.

Sifa is in open alpha. Profiles and LinkedIn import work today. We are building the rest in public.

Where this goes

As the network grows, something powerful becomes possible. An organization looking for Kubernetes expertise will be able to find people with actual, verified activity in Kubernetes communities. Not someone who typed a keyword on their profile. Real expertise, discovered through real contributions. That is what we are building toward.

Part of Singi Labs

Sifa is one of two products from Singi Labs, built by Guido Jansen in the Netherlands. Its sibling Barazo is a federated forum on the AT Protocol. The two feed into each other: your activity in Barazo communities shows up as verified contributions on your Sifa profile. Post where it matters, and your professional identity grows with you.
