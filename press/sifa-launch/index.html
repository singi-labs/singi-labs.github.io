Sifa ID: Professional identity on the AT Protocol

A complete picture of who you are. Powered by your network and own activity, not just your words.

Profiles should not be this broken

Your professional profile is flat and static. You type in your job titles. You write your own summary. You collect endorsements from people who may have never seen your work. None of it is connected to anything you actually do.

So the only way to stay visible is to post performative content on the platform itself. Thought leadership that nobody asked for. AI-generated thinkpieces spewed into the void. All to boost your findability on one platform, instead of contributing to the communities where the real work happens.

And then the platform asks you to pay to reach your own followers. The connections you built. Your colleagues, your community. "Boost your post to make sure the people that matter to you see it." That is not a feature. That is betrayal.

It is the same pattern everywhere. Platforms attract communities for free, earn their trust, onboard their members, then lock them in. Slack did it. Facebook Groups did it. Now LinkedIn is doing it.

What if your profile showed the whole picture?

Sifa ID is a professional identity layer on the AT Protocol. Sign in with your Bluesky account and claim your profile at sifa.id.

Your profile is not a form you fill out once and forget. On the AT Protocol, your data lives in one place: your PDS (personal data server). Sifa reads from it. Your Bluesky posts are already there. Your movie reviews on Popfeed. Your photos on Flashed. Your commits on Tangled. Your forum posts on Barazo. As more AT Protocol apps come online, your profile fills in on its own.

Just be part of the communities you care about. Curate what shows up if you want. Sifa takes care of the rest. No re-hashing your life for yet another platform.

Want to bring your existing career history? Import your LinkedIn profile in your browser. The data never leaves your device -- it goes straight to your PDS.

Sifa ID profile with career history, activity across the ATmosphere, and track record

Your community makes your profile real

On LinkedIn, it is just you polishing a page. Sifa works differently. Your profile gets stronger because of the people around you and how they respond to what you do.

Colleagues confirm where you work. Keytrace verifies your links. People you have actually met vouch for you through /meet. These trust signals come from real relationships, not a button click.

And then there is the activity itself. You help someone in a forum. You write a guide that people actually use. You answer questions. The community reacts, and that shows up on your profile. Not a summary you wrote about yourself. Actual contributions, confirmed by the people who were there.

"I managed communities for 25 years. I know what real contribution looks like, and it has never been reflected in a professional profile. The people doing real work in real communities deserve better than a static page they fill out themselves."
-- Guido Jansen, founder

Why now

25 million people already have an AT Protocol identity. The developer tooling caught up in early 2026. Nobody has published professional networking schemas for the protocol yet. We did.

Sifa is in open alpha. Profiles and LinkedIn import work today. We are building the rest in public.

Where this goes

Picture an organization looking for Kubernetes expertise. Today, they search profiles where people typed "Kubernetes" in a skills field. With Sifa, they could find someone who has been answering Kubernetes questions in three different communities for the past two years. That is what we are working toward.

Part of Singi Labs

Sifa is one of two products from Singi Labs, built by Guido X Jansen in the Netherlands. Its sibling Barazo is a federated forum on the AT Protocol. The two feed each other: what you do in Barazo communities shows up on your Sifa profile. Do good work where it matters, and your profile reflects it.
