Sifa: Professional identity on the AT Protocol

Your profile shows what you actually did. Verified.

Sifa is a professional identity layer for the AT Protocol. It tracks your real activity across AT Protocol apps and shows it as a verifiable professional profile. Not self-reported skills or meaningless endorsements, but actual contribution history tied to your cryptographic identity.

Show your work, not your claims

Social networks are filling up with AI-generated content and self-reported credentials. Sifa goes the other way: your profile shows real activity from real communities. Per-community contribution cards, verified identity through Keytrace, endorsements that are cryptographically signed by the endorser.

No reputation numbers. No tiers. No leaderboards. Just: here's what you did, here's who can vouch for it.

Trust infrastructure for the ecosystem

Sifa runs a Trust and Safety labeler using EigenTrust-based sybil detection. It publishes anti-abuse labels that any AT Protocol app can plug into. This runs internally for spam filtering and coordinated-abuse detection, but the scores are never surfaced to users. The point is protecting communities, not ranking people.

Trusted expertise discovery

Existing professional networks monetize people recruitment based on self-reported profiles. Sifa makes something else possible: trusted knowledge and skill recruitment based on verified contributions. An organization looking for Kubernetes expertise can find people with actual, verified activity in Kubernetes communities, not someone who typed a keyword on their profile.

Part of Singi Labs

Sifa is one of two products from Singi Labs. Its sibling Barazo is a federated forum platform on the AT Protocol. The two feed into each other: activity in Barazo communities builds your Sifa profile, and Sifa's trust labels protect Barazo communities in return.
