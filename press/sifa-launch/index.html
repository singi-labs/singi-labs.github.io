Sifa ID: A professional profile you don't have to fake

Professional identity on the AT Protocol. Powered by your network and real activity, not just your words.

Sifa ID is a professional identity layer built on the AT Protocol, the open network behind Bluesky. Instead of a static page you fill out yourself, your profile fills in from your real activity across apps and communities. Sifa ID is available today for anyone with a Bluesky account. 192 people have already claimed their profile. No Bluesky account yet? It takes two minutes, and that account works across every AT Protocol app.

Your online profile should not be this broken

Your professional profile is flat and static. You type in your job titles. You write your own summary. You collect endorsements from people who may have never seen your work. None of it is connected to anything you actually do.

So the only way to stay visible is to post performative content on the platform itself. Thought leadership that nobody asked for. AI-generated posts fired into the void. All to boost your findability on one platform, instead of contributing to the communities where the real work happens.

And then the platform asks you to pay to reach your own followers. The connections you built. Your colleagues, your community. "Boost your post to make sure the people that matter to you see it." That is not a feature. That is betrayal.

What if your profile showed the whole picture?

What if your profile was already being built, just by showing up and doing your work?

On the AT Protocol, your data lives in a personal data server that you control, not locked inside any single app. Sifa reads from it. Your Bluesky posts are already there, along with your forum contributions, code commits, photos, and event attendance. As more apps join the protocol, your profile fills in on its own.

Be part of the communities you care about and curate what shows up if you want. Sifa takes care of the rest. No re-hashing your life for yet another platform.

Want to bring your existing career history? Import your LinkedIn data in your browser. The data never leaves your device; it goes straight to your personal data server.

A Sifa ID profile: career history, real activity across apps, and a track record built by your network

Your network makes your profile credible

On LinkedIn, it is just you polishing a page. Sifa works differently. Your profile becomes more complete and more credible because of the people around you and how they respond to what you do.

Colleagues confirm where you work. Identity verification services prove your links are real. People you have met in person vouch for you. These trust signals come from real relationships, not a button click.

And then there is the activity itself. You help someone in a forum. You write a guide that people actually use. You answer questions. The community reacts, and that shows up on your profile: not a summary you wrote about yourself, but actual contributions confirmed by the people who were there.

"I managed communities for 25 years. I know what real contribution looks like, and it has never been reflected in a professional profile. The people doing real work in real communities deserve better than a static page they fill out themselves."
Guido X Jansen, founder

Why now

25 million people already have an AT Protocol identity, and the developer tooling caught up in early 2026. Nobody has defined how professional networking works on this protocol yet. I published the first open standard for it.

Profiles, LinkedIn import, activity feeds, endorsements, and verified accounts are live today. Full-text search and cross-app activity streams are coming next.

Where this goes

Picture an organization looking for Kubernetes expertise. Today, they search profiles where people typed "Kubernetes" in a skills field. With Sifa, they could find someone who has been answering Kubernetes questions in three different communities for the past two years.

Part of Singi Labs

Sifa is one of two products from Singi Labs, built by Guido X Jansen in the Netherlands. Its sibling Barazo is a distributed forum on the AT Protocol. The two feed each other: what you do in Barazo communities shows up on your Sifa profile. Do good work where it matters, and your profile reflects it.
